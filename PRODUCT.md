# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Car enthusiasts and everyday owners who keep their vehicles longer than the warranty period. They care about maintenance — not because a dashboard light is screaming, but because they want the car to last. Many own more than one vehicle. The interface meets them at moments of intent: after an oil change, before a long trip, when planning the next service.

## Product Purpose

GarageKeep is a personal garage and maintenance journal. Users log their cars, record services as they happen, and see at a glance what's overdue, what's coming up, and what's good. Success looks like the user opening the app once a month, knowing instantly what each car needs, and trusting that nothing has slipped through.

## Brand Personality

Quiet, considered, archival. Three words: **precise, warm, enduring**. The voice of a leather-bound service log, not a parts retailer. Numbers and facts speak first; the interface gets out of the way.

## Anti-references

- **AutoZone, Pep Boys, generic auto-retailer sites** — cluttered, loud reds, sale-banner clutter, stock illustrations.
- **Generic SaaS templates** — identical card grids, hero-metric dashboards, gradient CTA buttons, three-column feature blocks.
- **Crypto / AI dashboards** — neon on black, glow effects, holographic gradients, glassmorphism.

## Design Principles

1. **Numbers carry weight.** Mileage, dates, costs are the protagonists. Set them in display type with breathing room. The chrome around them stays quiet.
2. **Editorial calm over operator urgency.** Even "overdue" is rendered with restraint, a deep terracotta rather than a fire-engine red. The product never feels like an alarm clock.
3. **Motion as continuity, not decoration.** Transitions resolve where the eye is going. Soft ease-out, generous timing. Never spinning, never bouncing.
4. **One accent, used sparingly.** A single saturated color earns its appearances. Status colors stay muted and named, not pure RGB.
5. **The car is the photograph.** Imagin.studio renders are the visual heart of every car card. They get the space they deserve.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. All status communication uses both color and a text label, never color alone (color-blind users). Animation respects `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`, where motion drops to opacity-only fades. Hit targets >= 44px. Keyboard navigation across every interactive element with visible focus rings tuned to the bone palette.
